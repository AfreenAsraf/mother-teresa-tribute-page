Mother Teresa

Dedicated Humanitarian and Nobel Peace Prize Winner

About Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa, known as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, now North Macedonia. She dedicated her life to serving the impoverished and sick in the slums of Kolkata, India.

At the age of 18, she joined the Sisters of Loreto and later founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, providing care for the terminally ill, abandoned children, and the destitute.

Legacy and Achievements

Mother Teresa's selfless service and compassion earned her numerous awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. Her legacy lives on through the Missionaries of Charity, an organization she founded that continues to provide aid and comfort to the needy worldwide.

Her unwavering commitment to helping others has inspired countless individuals to follow in her footsteps and serve humanity.
